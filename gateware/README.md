# 🚀 smiSDR FPGA Core: Hardware-Accelerated I/Q Mixing

Welcome to the **`gateware`** subdirectory of the smiSDR project! 

This folder is dedicated to taking the smiSDR ecosystem to the next level. We are transitioning from pushing raw, high-rate RF data generated by software to a smarter, hardware-accelerated architecture. Soon, we will publish **generalized Verilog code** here, designed to transform a wide variety of FPGAs into high-performance SDR frontends.

## 🌟 The Concept: Why offload to an FPGA?

However, purely software-driven mixing relies heavily on host CPU limits. By shifting the heavy lifting to dedicated FPGA hardware, we unlock massive potential:

* **I/Q Over the smiBus:** Instead of streaming bandwidth-heavy raw RF, we transfer baseband **I/Q data** via the smiBus. This drastically reduces the required bus bandwidth while maintaining pristine signal integrity.
* **Hardware-Level DUC & DDC:** Once the I/Q data hits the FPGA, our generalized Verilog core takes over. It performs the **Digital Up-Conversion (DUC)** for transmission and **Digital Down-Conversion (DDC)** for reception right on the silicon.
* **Massive Frequency Range:** Freed from the processing bottlenecks of pure software NCOs, this hardware-accelerated mixing allows us to cover a significantly broader frequency spectrum.

🛠️ Supported & Tested Hardware

We designed this Verilog IP to be as generalized and portable as possible. You don't need a massive, overpriced development board to join the fun.

Initial testing has already proven highly successful across a diverse lineup of budget-friendly and mid-range FPGA families:

    🌪️ Intel / Altera: Cyclone IV, Cyclone 10, and the ultra-compact MAX 1000 boards.

    🧊 Lattice: Various iCEboards – perfect for those who love fully open-source synthesis toolchains.

    ⚡ Gowin: A wide array of Sipeed Tang Nano and Tang Primer boards.
